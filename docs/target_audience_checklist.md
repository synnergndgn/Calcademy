# Target Audience Checklist

Calcademy is positioned as an academic calculation workspace for high-school, university, engineering, statistics, finance, and operations-research users. Educational classification does not automatically mean the app is designed for children.

Official reference: [Manage target audience and app content settings](https://support.google.com/googleplay/android-developer/answer/9867159). Recheck it immediately before submission.

## Proposed positioning

- Not specifically designed for children.
- Primary users: older high-school students, university students, educators, engineers, analysts, and adult learners.
- Store copy should emphasize academic/professional study tools, not “kids,” “children,” cartoons, or child-directed rewards.
- The final selected Play age groups must reflect actual product design and local definitions of a child.

## Decision checklist — blocking

- [ ] Define the intended minimum audience age and countries of distribution.
- [ ] Select only age groups Calcademy was deliberately designed for.
- [ ] Confirm whether ages 13–15 and/or 16–17 are intended; these users can still be children under regional law.
- [ ] Do not include under-13 groups merely because mathematics is taught at those ages.
- [ ] Confirm store copy, icon, graphics, and screenshots do not contradict the declared audience.
- [ ] Record whether Calcademy is **not designed for children**.
- [ ] Have the release owner review the current Families Policy implications before saving the form.

## If any child audience is selected later

- Perform a complete Families Policy review.
- Reassess privacy policy, Data Safety, consent, identifiers, SDKs, links, and data practices.
- If ads exist, use only eligible Families Self-Certified Ads SDKs and configure child/unknown-age treatment correctly.
- Review all store media and language for child-directed signals.
- Consider whether a neutral age screen is required and ensure it does not encourage users to misstate age.
- Obtain appropriate legal/policy advice for target regions.

## Store-language guardrails

Avoid child-directed positioning unless it is an intentional, compliant product decision:

- “for kids,” “children's calculator,” “fun for toddlers,” or similar terms;
- child-focused mascots/reward loops used as primary merchandising;
- claims that homework answers are guaranteed.

Safe current direction: “academic calculation workspace for students, educators, and engineering-focused problem solving.”

## Final verification

- [ ] Privacy policy is available before completing target-audience settings.
- [ ] App access and ads declarations are completed first where Play Console requires them.
- [ ] Content rating and target-audience responses are mutually consistent.
- [ ] The decision is revisited before any AdMob/analytics/account sprint.

This checklist is product/policy guidance, not legal advice or a guarantee of Families eligibility.
